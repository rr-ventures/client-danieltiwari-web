Personal self-assessment

Taking an honest look

I created this assessment for you to gain insight over yourself and your life.

10–15 minutes

1 of 4
01

Your life, right now.

Rate each area on your first honest instinct. Don't overthink it.

How to answer this section — Daniel Tiwari

How to answer

Score each area based on your immediate gut feeling — not where you want to be, not where you think you should be. The only valuable answer is the honest one.

02

What matters to you.

Regardless of where each area stands right now — how important is it to you in life, long term?

1 = not important10 = extremely important
03

What's pressing right now.

How urgent does it feel to address each area — regardless of long-term importance?

1 = not urgent10 = highly urgent
04

Where to focus.

Pick 1, 2, or 3 areas to go deeper on.

Consider which areas, if improved, would have the biggest ripple effect on everything else in your life.
03

The deeper layer.

Choose the answer that is most honest, not the answer that sounds best.

How to answer this section — Daniel Tiwari

How to answer

Choose the option that most honestly describes where you are right now — not where you hope to be, and not the answer that sounds most self-aware. These questions are designed to cut through the stories we tell ourselves.

If two answers feel close, pick the one that makes you slightly uncomfortable. That discomfort usually means you're being accurate.

04

Fit signals.

This section helps separate real readiness from curiosity.

How to answer this section — Daniel Tiwari

How to answer

This section is not about qualifying yourself. It is about getting honest with yourself about what you actually want and what you are actually willing to do.

For the open questions at the end, write the version you would usually edit out. The unfiltered answer is almost always the more useful one — and the one I actually read.
