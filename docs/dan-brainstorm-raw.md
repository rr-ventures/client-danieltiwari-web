## Why each life area matters

### Career & Vocation

So, career is very important because in the game of being human in 2026 what was once hunt and gather has now become substitued by money. Everybody needs to earn money. And earning money in most cases takes up a great deal of our time, energy, and head space, which makes it even more important. And the way we in most cases earn money is through our career/work. Also, if you value freedom, you want to earn as much money as you can through ideally just being YOU. Getting paid for BEING YOU and DOING WHAT YOU DO. My definition of freedom is that you never have to do anything you don't want to do and can always do anything you do want to do.

### Romance

I guess this is a biological desire that at least almost every man has. Furthermore, men and women are a perfect team that complement each other perfectly, enabling a powerful synergy.

### Friendships

Friendships also a biological desire. Very important, especially for men I believe.

### Family

Family is a nice to have, but it's not something we can choose. We kind of have to work with what we've got but can do our best to have good and honest relationships. When it comes to procreation and having a family of our own, it's probably one of the most meaningful things a human being can experience. However, it shouldn't be created out of boredom or meaninglessness in life.

### Health & Vitality

Obviously one of the most important foundations for any successful life as a human being. Often times underestimated and undervalued. People sacrifice health to save money while they are healthy, and end up spending money to get it back once it's gone. When you're healthy you have a lot of problems. When you're sick you only have one.

### Attractiveness / Appearance

To a great extent being attractive and sexy is internal. However, there's nothing wrong with maximizing looks and grooming properly. I think the basics are more than worth being taken care of always (grooming, haircut, basic clothes, and a decent physique etc.).

### Meaning & Spirituality

Very important in order to play a successful, enjoyable, and sustainable human game.

## Vision (focus area deeper question)

So, having a vision is really important in life. And this isn't about just having some vision. It's about having a vision that is both attractive/engaging/exciting for us, while also being a vision that we believe is achievable (even if extremely challenging or only a 1% chance). If those two things are not given, then it's like an electricity circuit that is not closed and therefore the electricity (motivation/energy/drive) doesn't flow, and the light bulb does not glow. This vision is like the destination you type into google maps. If you don't have a destination that is attractive, you're not going to want to put your pedal to the floor and get there ASAP. If the destination isn't perceived as achievable, then it's like having a destination that has no way to get there, so nothing happens. Often times, the reason people have addictions or don't want freedom is because they don't have a vision, and the lack of vision creates a state of meaninglessness and inactivity, which can often times be worse than being enslaved in a 9 to 5 job etc. with a tyrannical boss or government. It's like in the story of exodus, where the slaves are freed from slavery and are all happy at first because they think they are now finally free and will enter the promise land, but instead they land in the desert first where they lack all orientation and meaning and values. No capacity for self-governance. No vision of their own. I have at least two if not 3 clips where Jordan Peterson speaks on this that I will want to add to the PDF to this block for the user if his vision is either "Partially" or "No, not yet". Also, in this case I want them to know that just because they might not know in their conscious mind what they want, they know it subconsciously, and their vision just needs to be uncovered. If they didn't have a vision at all, then there would be nothing to contrast the state of unfulfillment they're in. The fact that they are unfulfilled implies that there is a state in which they would be fulfilled and that implies whatever their vision is. I like the image of a dinosaur skeletton that archeologists uncover with a brush in the desert sand. Another analogy is like putting together pieces of a puzzle. As you move through life and gain new experiences, each experience has the potential to hold a valuable puzzle piece for you if you are reflective, curious, and honest with yourself. At some point you can put together the puzzle pieces and you will have a clear and understandable picture of who you are and what your vision is. Steve jobs said that life is like connect the dots but looking backwards. I have a speech of his where he talks about this that I will also want to add to the pdf if it's relevant for the user. Also, although it's not quite as potent as knowing what you want, a lot of people at least know what they don't want, and that is a great place to start. The reason knowing what you don't want isn't that potent is because it's motivated by negativity and fear. It's like you're being pushed away from something, whereas if you know what you want, it's motivated by joy and love and passion, and it's like you're being pulled towards it. So, it's so important to be clear on your vision. Without it, you are not able to live and invest your time and energy (which are your most valuable resources in life) with intention. You will instead just be randomly drifting on sea, governed by other people and circumstances and will never arrive at the destination you want to arrive at.

Also, another thing to add to the vision brainstorm is that not knowing what you want is NEVER a reason for depression or passivity or inactivity in life. These things often use supposedly not having a vision as a mask to hide themselves. Not knowing what you want only becomes a bad thing if you feel pressure into having to know what you want (for whatever reasons), or if you're just looking for a reason to stay inactive, passive, in your comfort zone, or pursue whatever it is you ACTUALLY want but have not admitted to yourself that you want it. Theoretically, a healthy and life-affirming person could approach not knowing what they want with excitement and enthusiasm and curiosity and adventure, with the attitude of something along the lines of "Ohhh, how cool is it that I don't know what I want. That means I get to try stuff out and find out what I like and don't like, what an adventure, this is going to be exciting and fun!!!!!".

A lot of times, if our vision isn't actually what we want but only what we THINK we want or think we're SUPPOSED TO want, it doesn't fuel us with energy/drive/motivation. In fact, it may even drain us or cause problems for us if what we ACTUALLY WANT is in conflict with that vision. So in the best case scenario, if our vision isn't actually ours, it simply won't fuel us with energy. In the worse case, we may even be working against ourselves if that vision is in conflict with what we actually want or our true values. The entirety of society has always told us what to do, who to be, and what to want. Imperatives are everywhere, while questionmarks are very rare (haha, that rhymes). Therefore, it is really common for many, even in their adult ages to not even know what they want or to still be pursuing a vision that actually isn't theirs, and therefore pursuing it with a very low amount of energy and motivation. It's an aspect of the quote on my hero headline, which is that most people are wasting their lives trying to be someone they're not, instead of excelling as who they are.

#### Anxiety

(Content pulled out via a Socratic client-roleplay exercise — Dan playing coach, Claude playing a client who checked "anxiety." Dan's questions to draw it out: "Why are you so anxious?" / "What do you mean with 'something bad'? How bad can it get?" / "How bad can existence really get?" / "What happens if you fuck it up?" / "Why is that such a big deal?" / "What is there to count in the midst of infinity?" / "What else would you want to do for eternity if you were god?")

You have to come to grips with the fact that ultimately, what's going to happen is going to happen. You're not fully in control of what happens so you need to let go a bit and find some acceptance for whatever happens. Some faith. Letting go of the desire to control everything.

The anxiety is arising because you are taking life and existence (too) seriously. You lack a metaphysical context in which life is happening that allows for you to be easy going and playful rather than taking things overly serious and therefore being anxious.

It's the dream of life by Alan Watts. Or thinking about our technological advancement with television etc — it's all attempting to reproduce reality so that we can have a game that feels real but isn't real. The perfect compromise between seriousness and a game. The middle ground in which we take it seriously enough for it to be a good game, but not too seriously so that we are too anxious to play.

The point of philosophy is to enhance your practical life. Your philosophy or your metaphysical foundation is entirely deciding on what your life experience is. This isn't just philosophizing for fun — this actually changes your experience of life. There are probably many ways of dealing with and coping with anxiety, but the only real solution is considering whether existence is actually serious or not (hint: according to logic, it's not, but that's something the ego cannot know for sure).

Quote to include: Alan Watts — "Man suffers only because he takes seriously what the gods made for fun."

Furthermore, the experience of anxiety itself isn't even the ultimate problem. The ultimate problem is that we experience everything from an overly serious perspective, and that's why anxiety, which has been associated negatively in this society, is experienced in a very unpleasant way. But if you were to imagine that this life is a game and the human being you are is the character you're playing, then anxiety is just information. It's a signal. And if you don't take existence as overly serious, then you can even experience anxiety in a not so unpleasant way. And it also won't spiral out of control by becoming anxious about feeling anxious and becoming anxious about feeling anxious about feeling anxious etc., a vicious feedback loop.

So, ultimately, the cure to anxiety is taking pressure out of the system. Whether you do that with lifestyle changes (good sleep, good diet, exercise, social life, and taking care of the things that need to be taken care of in your life), or through a metaphysical foundation, or whatever else may exist that can help. Ideally the vicious cycle needs to be stopped first, and once that's gone, the initial anxiety can be dealt with as well. Acceptance, faith, humility and surrender play a role in this.
