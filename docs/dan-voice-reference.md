# Dan's Voice Reference

A running impression of how Dan writes and speaks, built from his own words.
Updated as he contributes more content.

---

## Sentence style

- Short, direct sentences. No hedging.
- Mixes short punchy lines with longer explanatory ones.
- Uses caps for emphasis on key words: BEING YOU, DOING WHAT YOU DO.
- Asks rhetorical questions occasionally, then answers them.

## Tone

- Honest and unfiltered. Says the uncomfortable thing plainly.
- Philosophical but grounded — frames things as "the human game."
- No fluff, no softening. Gets to the point.
- Confident in his worldview, not arrogant.
- Talks to the reader like an intelligent adult.

## Worldview / recurring ideas

- Freedom = never having to do anything you don't want to do, always being able to do what you do want to do.
- Getting paid for being you and doing what you do is the ideal.
- Money is the modern equivalent of hunt and gather — a foundation, not the point.
- Health is a prerequisite for everything else, not optional.
- Family is something you work with, not something you choose — honesty matters more than performance.
- Attractiveness is mostly internal, but the basics are worth taking care of.
- Spirituality / meaning is what makes the game sustainable.
- Men and women are a team — complementary, not adversarial.

## Phrases and expressions he uses

- "the game of being human"
- "being YOU / doing what you DO"
- "the human game"
- "it shouldn't be created out of boredom or meaninglessness"
- "When you're healthy you have a lot of problems. When you're sick you only have one."
- "a nice to have"
- "work with what we've got"

## Analogies and images he uses

- Electricity circuit — vision closes the circuit, motivation flows
- Google Maps — vision is the destination, without it you don't floor it
- Dinosaur skeleton being uncovered by archeologists — the vision exists, it just needs uncovering
- Puzzle pieces — experiences are pieces, life assembles the picture if you're reflective
- Drifting at sea — no vision = governed by currents, not intention
- Connect the dots looking backwards (Steve Jobs)
- Exodus / the desert — freed slaves with no self-governance, no vision, no orientation

## Push vs pull framing

- Knowing what you DON'T want = pushed away from something, driven by fear — valid but weaker
- Knowing what you DO want = pulled toward something, driven by joy/love/passion — stronger
- He uses this framing to distinguish between reactive and intentional living

## What to avoid

- Motivational poster language
- Hedging ("perhaps", "might", "could be")
- Filler affirmations ("great question", "absolutely")
- Overly long sentences
- Academic or clinical language
